Why Zarafa Camp?

Why Zarafa Camp?

Experience

Zarafa Camp overlooks the magnificent Zibadianja Lagoon – the source of the world-famous Savute Channel.The Selinda Reserve is famous for its massive herds of elephants & large populations of predators, such as lion, leopard, cheetah & wild dog. Abundant plains game can also be found here, including kudu, buffalo, zebra & giraffe.

Stay

Zarafa Camp consists of only 4 luxuriously appointed safari tents. Hosting a maximum of 8 guests, the emphasis at Zarafa Camp is on offering guests a private or exclusive safari experience.Each tent has a private veranda, positioned under shady trees with breathtaking views of the Zibadianja Lagoon. The bedrooms are large & have en-suite bathrooms, featuring a copper bath as well as indoor & outdoor showers.

Zarafa Camp is furnished in 20th-century décor & finishes. The main area consists of a lounge & dining area – both set under canvas as well as a large viewing deck shaded by giant trees.

Activities

Activities at Zarafa include morning, afternoon & night drives in open 4x4 safari vehicles as well as guided bush walks. Guests will be accompanied by professional guides who will introduce them to some of Africa’s most interesting wildlife.Another way to relax & experience all Africa has to offer, is by enjoying a traditional African massage in the privacy of your tent.

Have you ever dreamed of an authentic, off-the-beaten-track wilderness experience? The Great Plains Expeditions offers a 4-night (set departure) Botswana cross country safari through the private Selinda Reserve in either an easterly or westerly direction.

Selinda Camp

Selinda Camp overlooks the Selinda Spillway, a unique seasonal waterway that links the Okavango Delta with the Linyanti Swamps. Packed with some of Africa’s wildest animals, the region enjoys an abundance of game, including predators, antelope &amp; over 300 bird species.

Selinda Explorers Camp

Selinda Explorers Camp overlooks the Selinda Spillway, a unique seasonal waterway that links the Okavango Delta with the Linyanti Swamps. Its sister camp, Selinda, is located about one hour west. Packed with some of Africa’s wildest animals, the region enjoys an abundance of game, including predators, antelope &amp; over 300 bird species.
