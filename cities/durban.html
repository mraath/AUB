Why Durban?

Why Durban?

Experience

The friendly city of Durban lies at the heart of the east coast & is a wonderful springboard from which to access KwaZulu-Natal’s most popular destinations, including the Drakensberg, iSimangaliso Wetland Park & Phinda Game Reserve.The multi-cultural city with its large Indian & Zulu populations is home to warm water beaches, high-rise hotels & the world’s 5th largest aquarium: Ushaka Marine World and so many other things to see and do.Durban has a wide selection of bars & restaurants, offering a diverse menu that will suit every palate. Here you can enjoy the best Indian curries in South Africa, seafood fresh from the ocean & the ever-popular bunny chow – a South African fast food dish consisting of a hollowed-out loaf of white bread filled with curry.Unlike many of South Africa’s other beach destinations, Durban boasts warm water – so swimmers & surfers are likely to spend much of their beach time immersed in the Indian Ocean.

Activities

The Golden Mile is one of the top attractions in Durban, & as such, is certainly worth exploring. As its name implies, this beachfront promenade boasts a gorgeous stretch of several golden beaches & on your doorstep you will find some of Durban’s best restaurants, shops & cultural attractions.Ushaka Marine World – the world’s 5th largest aquarium should certainly be added to your list of activities. Marvel at the underwater world & all its interesting creatures, get wet at the waterpark, enjoy thrilling animal encounters or challenge yourself at the rope adventure park.Other popular Durban activities include taking a stroll through the botanical gardens or shopping at Victoria Street Market with its wide selection of African artwork, Indian spices, food & traditional clothes.

When to travel

Durban enjoys warm weather year-round. Summer is between November to April when the city experiences its rainy season - but days are warm & humid with average temperatures around 28 degrees Celsius.Our best Durban travel recommendation is that for many people, Durban is the perfect winter destination. It is still warm from May to October, with the average temperature around 23 degrees Celsius, even in the coldest parts of the year.

Lodges
Canelands Beach Club & Sp...

The laidback Canelands Beach Club &amp; Spa boasts a spectacular location overlooking the beach at Salt Rock on the subtropical Kwazulu-Natal coast. The charming town of Ballito is nearby &amp; King Saka International Airport is only a 15-minute drive away, making this an ideal base from which to explore the surrounding area or as an overnight stay en route to the Battlefields or Phinda Game Reserve.

Teremok Marine Lodge

Teremok Marine Lodge is situated in beautiful Umhlanga, one of the most popular beach destinations on Durban’s north coast. Located near a handful of excellent swimming beaches &amp; within driving distance of popular attractions like uShaka Marine World &amp; Gateway shopping complex, Teremok is an ideal base from which to explore the surrounding area.

The Oyster Box

The stylish Oyster Box is perched on the edge of the Indian Ocean &amp; offers discerning travellers an elegant retreat close to many of Durban’s top attractions. The hotel is a fusion of old-world charm &amp; modern-day convenience, boasting 2 swimming pools (one with an ocean view &amp; the other one in the gardens that is for over 12’s only), a spa with jacuzzi &amp; hammam as well as a fully-equipped fitness centre.

Zimbali Resort

Zimbali Resort is situated inside the elite Zimbali Coastal Estate on the beautiful Dolphin Coast in Kwazulu-Natal. The resort boasts sensational views of the Indian Ocean &amp; offers its guests a 5-star service with the greatest attention to detail.
